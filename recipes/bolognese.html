Bolognese Sauce

Description

This traditional Italian sauce is a family favorite. The fattiness of the beef and milk combines with the acidity of the tomatoes and earthiness of the vegetables to create a dish that is perfect for hot summer days or cozy winter evenings. The recipe can be easily doubled, tripled, or quadrupled, making it perfect for large gatherings - I have premade three portions to take on our 20-person family camping trips! Once you reach the simmer stage, low and slow is best. By the time it's done, your house will smell delicious.

Ingredients

  • 1 tbsp extra virgin olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 yellow onion
  • 1 red bell pepper
  • 2 stalks celery
  • 2 carrots, sliced, diced, or slivered
  • 1 lb ground beef (substitute turkey if preferred)
  • 1/8 tsp nutmeg
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 1 cup dry white wine
  • 1 15oz can tomatoes, whole or diced
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 lb tagliatelle (can substitute fettuccine, spaghetti, or rigatoni)
  • Parmesan cheese
  • Salt
  • Pepper

Steps

  1. In a Dutch oven or other large pan, heat garlic in oil over medium heat until simmering.
  2. Add onion, chopped, and cook until transluscent.
  3. Add celery, carrot, and bell pepper. Cook until slightly softened, about 2-3 minutes. Stir regularly.
  4. Add meat, pinch of salt, and a few twists of a pepper grinder. Stir the meat to break apart and cook until brown.
  5. Turn down heat. While pan cools slightly, warm milk in microwave or on stove until nearly boiling. This will prevent the milk from curdling.
  6. Add the milk. Adjust heat until it is just simmering. Simmer until the liquid has evaporated and milkfat has absorbed into beef (may take 15-20 minutes at low heat).
  7. Stir in the nutmeg.
  8. Add the wine, simmer in a similar fashion to the milk.
  9. Add tomatoes & stir thoroughly. Turn stove down to the lightest simmer and leave for at least one hour, preferably three, ideally six or more.
  10. Serve over freshly cooked pasta with freshly grated Parmesan cheese.
